PLANT CLASSIFICATION USING LEAVES

Correctly classifying plant species is crucial for ecological and conservation efforts, yet traditional machine learning approaches rely on manually extracted features, which can be limiting. Our study focuses on automating the classification of 10 plant species native to India using advanced deep learning techniques, specifically employing models like ResNet-50, EfficientNet-B5, VGG16, and InceptionNet-V3. We found that ResNet-50 outperformed the other models, significantly improving the precision and effectiveness of plant classification tasks. This research not only addresses the challenges posed by India’s diverse plant species but also paves the way for future applications in agriculture, horticulture, and environmental science.

Automatic Number Plate Recognition

This project focuses on the automatic detection of vehicles and recognition of their license plates from video frames, leveraging cutting-edge computer vision techniques. To achieve this, we utilize the YOLOv8 model for vehicle detection, employing the pre-trained version (yolov8n.pt) trained on the COCO dataset, which effectively identifies various vehicle classes, including cars, buses, and trucks. Following vehicle detection, we employ a fine-tuned YOLOv8 model (best.pt) specifically designed to locate license plates within the detected vehicles’ bounding boxes. To maintain consistent tracking of vehicles across video frames, we implement the SORT algorithm, ensuring accurate identification even as vehicles move or frames change. Finally, we utilize EasyOCR for text recognition, extracting license plate numbers from cropped images by converting them to grayscale, applying thresholding for enhanced clarity, and accurately reading the text from the processed images. This integrated approach not only streamlines vehicle identification but also enhances the reliability of license plate recognition in real-time applications

Youtube Notes Maker

This application simplifies the process of summarizing YouTube videos by leveraging LangChain and the youtube_transcript_api. It begins by retrieving the video transcript, which is then processed into coherent text paragraphs. Using LangChain’s PromptTemplate, these paragraphs are formatted and sent to a language model (LLM) to generate concise, summarized notes. The results are displayed on a Streamlit-based web interface using markdown, offering a clean and accessible way for users to quickly capture the key points from YouTube videos.
